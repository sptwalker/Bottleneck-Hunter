# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Chinese retail investors and independent researchers doing fundamental and thematic supply chain analysis. They are semi-professional — comfortable with financial data, sector concepts, and stock screeners, but not quant-level. Typical context: evening research session at a desk, focused and methodical, comparing sectors and suppliers before making investment decisions.

## Product Purpose

BottleneckHunter is an AI-powered supply chain bottleneck stock screener. It decomposes industry chains, identifies scarce/irreplaceable upstream suppliers, and cross-validates findings across multiple LLMs. The goal is to surface overlooked "chokepoint" suppliers that the market has not yet priced in — the inverse of chasing popular terminal stocks.

## Brand Personality

Professional, Reliable, Efficient. The tool should feel like a trusted research assistant — authoritative data presentation, zero fluff, fast to results. Confidence without arrogance.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with hero metrics and gradient cards
- Bloomberg Terminal raw density without visual hierarchy
- Overly playful or gamified investment apps (Robinhood aesthetic)
- AI-generated "cream/sand" warm neutral palettes

## Design Principles

1. **Data speaks first** — Every pixel serves information density or readability. Decoration that doesn't aid comprehension is removed.
2. **Progressive depth** — Show the headline, let the user drill into the detail. Top-level summary → ranked table → per-item deep dive.
3. **Pipeline transparency** — The 5-step analysis is a journey. Show where you are, what's done, what's next.
4. **Trust through precision** — Exact numbers, clear sources, multi-model validation badges. Vague language erodes credibility.
5. **Bilingual-ready** — Primary language is Chinese; all labels and copy must work in both zh and en.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Color must not be the sole indicator of state (pass/concern/fail). Support reduced motion. Consider users with color vision deficiency for chart/score color coding.
